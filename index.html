Welcome

Addison McGhee is a graduate of UW-Madison Statistics (BS '20) and Harvard Biostatistics (MS '23). As an undergraduate, he worked with Dr. Christine Wilson-Mendenhall at the UW-Madison Center for Healthy Minds, where he analyzed patient writing samples to detect relationships among prounoun use and symptoms of depression/anxiety. During graduate school, Addison interned for over a year at the clinical trial firm Repare Therapeutics. His work with Repare involved modeling (longitudinally) patient hemoglobin levels using mixed-effects models and maintaining an R Shiny dashboard (~6000 lines of R code).

Work Experience

Currently I'm on the New Person Experience (NPX) team under the Growth Engineering org. My team owns and optimizes the onboarding process for Facebook. Day to day, I mostly program in Java for the Android app, and PHP (Hack) for our backend.

At Google's Nest Labs, I worked on the Data Infrastruture team in Palo Alto, California from May to August 2016. I built a Dataflow job monitoring dashboard in Python/Django hosted on Google App Engine.

At Microsoft I worked on the Payment Services team in Redmond, Washington from May-August 2015. I built and optimized an ASP.NET Web API for a new payment monitoring dashboard.

|

At Commonwealth Computer Research Inc., I worked on a contract for the Office of Naval Resarch. I wrote an algorithm in Scala which used NLP to parse and geocode location names found in news articles. You can read more about what I did on the CCRi company blog
